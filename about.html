About us

History of MARELLA

Marella International Group Schools, founded in 1979 by Late Mrs. Maria De'Lourdes Adebisi, started as a playgroup with just two students. Over time, it expanded to include a nursery/primary school in 1983, and eventually a college in 1997. In 1999, the proprietress retired and handed over the reins to Edward and Michelle Adebisi, who maintain high standards and a 6 to 1 student-teacher ratio. The school has two locations, Oluyole Estate for Creche, Nursery, and Primary, and Kongi, Bodija for High school and A level classes, with excellent dormitories. Marella alumni have received scholarships and excelled in various universities and extracurricular activities, including basketball, swimming, and more.

Our Mission

At Marella International School, our mission is to inspire and empower students to become lifelong learners, critical thinkers, and responsible global citizens. We are committed to providing a nurturing and inclusive educational environment that fosters academic excellence, creativity, and character development. Through innovative teaching practices and a diverse, collaborative community, we strive to prepare our students to excel in an ever-changing world.

Our Vision

Our vision at Marella International School is to be a beacon of educational excellence, recognized locally and globally for our commitment to holistic education. We envision a school where every student discovers their unique potential, embraces diversity, and contributes positively to society.
